"Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance" is a philosophical and autobiographical work by Robert M. Pirsig that explores the author's motorcycle journey with his son, interwoven with his philosophical musings on the concept of "quality." Pirsig delves into the intersection of Eastern Zen philosophy and Western rationality, offering a reflection on the pursuit of quality and the nature of value in life. He examines the conflict between the romantic, intuitive approach to understanding the world and the classical, analytical perspective. Throughout the book, Pirsig embarks on a philosophical inquiry, highlighting the importance of finding a harmonious balance between these contrasting worldviews to lead a more meaningful and fulfilling life. It ultimately invites readers to contemplate the nature of human experience and the search for quality in everyday existence.
